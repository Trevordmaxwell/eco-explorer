# 2026-03-30 Inland Route Phase

## Recommendation

The next highest-leverage move is to activate the inland half of the season board.

The game now has:

- one live field-season board
- one coastal route
- stronger coastal and forest content support
- a treeline shelter proof
- a small third station utility
- visible revisit change across the whole chain

That means the next phase should not be another disconnected feature.

It should be the second live route: a more alpine, shorter-season line that makes `forest`, `treeline`, and `tundra` feel like part of one authored inland chapter.

## Why This Is The Right Shift

The current build now has a strong early and mid-coastal game loop.

What is still weaker is:

- the inland branch as a longer authored route
- the feeling that the back half of the world matters as much as the front half
- medium-term route progression after the first coastal line resolves

The next leverage is to turn the inland branch into a true second chapter, not just a set of available biomes.

## Recommended Order

1. activate the second live route on the field-season board
2. add alpine route content fuel
3. add one tundra traversal or shelter proof
4. add a compact route-completion summary or atlas acknowledgment
5. add quiet corridor-authored route beats for inland links

## Phase 1. Second Live Route

The best next live route is the inland line previously hinted by scout work.

Strong candidate:

- `Treeline Shelter Line`, with `Short Season Line` still kept as a later follow-on

Best shape:

- start from the field station board
- use existing forest and treeline supports first
- point toward tundra as the route’s later reveal
- keep the route notebook-first and medium in length, not giant or checklist-heavy

This should feel like:

- moving from canopy cover toward exposure
- noticing how plants and shelter strategies change
- reaching a colder, shorter-season world without changing the tone of the game

## Phase 2. Alpine Route Content Fuel

The second route will need more content support than the coast did at the start.

Best additions:

- one or two stronger treeline discoveries tied to shelter and exposure
- one or two tundra discoveries tied to short season timing, thaw, or low growth
- one more comparison-worthy shared species or habitat-role contrast
- one additional note or route-support prompt in the inland branch

This will matter because the second route should feel authored, not like the player is just repeating the coastal structure in colder colors.

## Phase 3. Tundra Traversal Proof

After the second route shell and its content fuel are holding, deepen `tundra` with one short authored movement proof.

Good proof directions:

- thaw-pool skirt
- frost-heave step route
- one low sheltered depression

Guardrails:

- short and readable
- no harsh failure loop
- one compact movement lesson, not a bigger terrain rewrite

## Phase 4. Route Completion Summary

Once two routes exist, the field station should acknowledge them more clearly.

Best scope:

- one tiny route-complete summary
- one atlas-like line or route badge
- one clear next suggestion

Do not turn this into:

- a big dashboard
- a collectibles wall
- a score ladder

## Phase 5. Inland Corridor Beats

Only after the inland route exists should the inland corridor links gain their own subtle authored route beats.

Best targets:

- `forest <-> treeline`
- `treeline <-> tundra`

That work should stay quiet:

- one or two authored carrier discoveries
- one prompt window or note-backed comparison window
- one readable travel cue

The corridor should still teach mostly through the world itself.

## Scout Work Worth Running In Parallel

1. inland route beat sheet and pacing
2. tundra traversal concept pack
3. route-completion acknowledgment framing

## Durable Rules

- The second route should make the back half of the world feel authored, not just unlocked.
- Keep one live inland route at a time instead of opening several equal-weight route tracks immediately.
- The station board should remain compact and notebook-like.
- Tundra traversal should stay low, shelter-aware, and short-season in feeling.
- Route completion should feel like fieldwork recognition, not a score screen.

- queue two more scout lanes for tundra traversal and route-completion framing
