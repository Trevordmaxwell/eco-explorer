# Tactile Living-World And Support Phase

## Summary

The next route-design step should make the living-world systems and support row matter more to play, not just to wording.

The review's strongest open questions are:

- can weather, day-part, phenology, and process moments change how an outing feels in practice?
- do helper choices produce genuinely different play, or are they mostly flavor?

Lane 4 should answer both without adding inventory sprawl or more shell density.

## Goals

- make one or two live routes feel tactically different under a living-world window
- use clue prominence, guidance, or route emphasis instead of hard gating
- either sharpen support-choice differentiation or keep the row intentionally tiny

## Wave Shape

### Step 1

- prepare the tactile living-world route handoff

### Step 2

- implement one route pass where living-world state changes clue reading or opportunity in play

### Step 3

- review whether the route now feels more alive instead of just relabeled

### Step 4

- prepare the support-differentiation follow-on handoff

### Step 5

- implement either stronger helper differentiation or a deliberate simplification

### Step 6

- review whether the support row now feels meaningfully different or appropriately minimal

## Guardrails

- no new inventory, quest log, or crafting layer
- prefer tactile feedback and route emphasis over more explanatory copy
- if a helper still does not create a felt difference, simplify instead of expanding
