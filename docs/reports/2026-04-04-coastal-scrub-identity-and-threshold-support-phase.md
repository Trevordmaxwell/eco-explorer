# Coastal Scrub Identity And Threshold Support Phase

## Summary

The latest review makes the product read clearly:

- forest is the flagship benchmark biome
- coastal scrub still risks feeling like connective tissue unless it gets a stronger identity pass
- treeline and tundra should lean harder into exposure, threshold, and seasonal harshness as readable play texture

Lane 2 should deepen those places through authored support content, not another new shell.

## Goals

- give coastal scrub stronger remembered identity through notes, close-look, comparison, and sketchbook support
- reinforce treeline and tundra as exposure-threshold spaces through compact authored content
- keep the screen visual-first and handheld-safe

## Wave Shape

### Step 1

- prepare the coastal scrub identity support handoff

### Step 2

- implement one content support pack that makes coastal scrub feel like a destination

### Step 3

- review whether coastal scrub now reads as a place, not just a corridor

### Step 4

- prepare the treeline and tundra threshold support handoff

### Step 5

- implement one compact second-act threshold support pack

### Step 6

- review whether exposure, shelter loss, and short-season clues are now more memorable

## Guardrails

- do not solve identity with more route-board copy
- prefer note-backed, visual-first, or comparison-backed support over longer prose
- keep support work modular and content-owned
