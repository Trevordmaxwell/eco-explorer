# Depth And Game-Feel Sequence

Date: 2026-03-28
Status: Approved future-direction plan

## Decision

The following are now approved long-term directions for Eco Explorer:

- gentle seasons and phenology shifts
- notebook-style relationship prompts instead of achievement quests
- shared-species comparison pages
- an art-forward sketchbook mode
- sparse, warm biome soundscapes
- occasional close-look inspect vignettes

These are not immediate implementation work. They are part of the durable app plan and should shape future scout and main-agent choices.

## Core Principle

Do not add systems just to add systems.

The right expansions are the ones that help the player notice:

- pattern
- change over time
- shelter and exposure
- repetition and difference across habitats
- how the same organism behaves differently in different places

That is where this game already feels special, so future depth should lean harder into it.

## Recommended Long-Term Order

### 1. Finish the current phase-two cleanup

Before the bigger expansions open up, the current journal and progression follow-ups should settle:

- local-sighting-aware shared-species progress
- dense journal page navigation

### 2. Ecology Over Time

This is the strongest next big family of features.

Recommended sequence:

1. day-part
2. weather
3. seasonal or phenology variation

Best first feeling:

- the world is in a different mood today

Not:

- a heavy simulation
- a farming-game calendar
- a pressure system

Good examples:

- spring bloom
- late-summer berries
- foggy coast mornings
- early treeline snow
- brief tundra flowering windows

### 3. Notebook Depth

After time and weather signals exist, deepen the educational loop through observation prompts and cross-habitat comparison.

Recommended sequence:

1. notebook-style relationship prompts
2. shared-species comparison pages

The prompt tone should stay like a naturalist notebook:

- `Find two signs of shelter.`
- `Notice one species that stays low to avoid wind.`
- `Compare one shared species across two habitats.`

These should not feel like chores, badges, or quiz prompts.

### 4. Player Authorship

Once the journal and comparison surfaces are stronger, add one authorship system:

- sketchbook mode

This should feel like arranging a field notebook spread, not crafting.

Good first scope:

- tiny sketches, stamps, or silhouettes
- drag-and-place discovered species and landmarks
- calm habitat pages the player composes

### 5. Sensory And Close-Look Depth

These are meaningful feel upgrades, but they should stay secondary to the observation loop.

Recommended sequence:

1. soundscape pass
2. close-look inspect mode

Sound should stay:

- spare
- warm
- biome-distinct

Close-look mode should stay:

- occasional
- delightful
- visually explanatory

Not every inspectable needs it.

## Relationship To Existing Parked Work

This plan extends the already-approved living-world sequence.

- `main-31` and `main-32` remain the first atmospheric steps
- `main-33` should now be read as relationship-first notebook prompts, not generic helper chatter
- the later systems here should follow that same observation-first tone instead of reopening score or quest design

## Scout Backlog

The scout can productively work on these in downtime without activating implementation:

1. seasons and phenology grounding
2. notebook-style relationship prompts
3. shared-species comparison pages
4. sketchbook mode v1
5. soundscape direction
6. close-look inspect mode

Each scout pass should stay practical:

- one small report
- concrete implementation guidance
- scope limits
- art and science guardrails

## Guardrails

- science accuracy remains a hard gate
- prompts should feel like noticing, not chores
- sketchbook work should feel expressive, not like crafting or housing
- sound should support calm exploration, not overpower it
- comparison pages should reinforce ecology and ecotones, not just repeat facts
- close-look panels should reduce text load when possible instead of adding more prose

## Queue Outcome

- keep the current main-agent work focused on the phase-two fixes
- open a scout-only planning queue for these future depth systems
- keep implementation of these ideas parked until the groundwork earns them
