# Post-Edge Pattern Phase

## Summary

With `Edge Pattern Line` now in active implementation, the next highest-leverage phase is not another unrelated feature. The best move is to make the route, nursery, front-half exploration, and station board feel like one stronger chapter of play.

This phase should do four things in order:

1. review the third route cleanly once it lands
2. deepen that route with transition-driven content fuel
3. add one short front-half sheltered traversal proof
4. tighten the field-station board so three routes still feel calm and episodic

## Why This Phase

- The nursery has already made the station more game-like.
- The third route is the first chance to make the coastal-to-inland transition branch feel authored instead of simply connected.
- The front half of the world still has less movement identity than the inland half.
- The station is now carrying enough route state that it needs a compact chapter rhythm, not just more rows.

## Direction

### 1. Edge Pattern Line review first

The first question is whether the third route actually reads as a transition chapter:

- nursery support should help, not gate
- the route board should stay compact at `256x160`
- coastal scrub, forest, and treeline should feel more connected by shelter, exposure, moisture, and edge patterns

### 2. Transition-driven content fuel

If the route shell is sound, the next pass should deepen it through content instead of a new system:

- one stronger coastal or transition discovery support
- one comparison or ecosystem-note anchor that teaches pattern change
- one or two route-aware prompt or note supports across `coastal-scrub`, `forest`, and `treeline`

This should strengthen the route as a teaching chapter, not just raise totals.

### 3. Front-half sheltered traversal proof

After the route content is stronger, the beach/coastal front half needs one short optional traversal proof so movement identity is not concentrated only inland.

Best fit:

- a dune-cut, wrack underpass, driftwood shelter, or low hollow

Guardrails:

- keep it curiosity-forward
- do not turn it into hard platforming
- do not generalize into a cave engine

### 4. Three-route station scaling

Once the third route and front-half traversal are both real, the field station needs one refinement pass:

- keep the route board primary
- keep the atlas and nursery secondary
- add one calm stopping-point signal so a route feels like an episode with a clean place to stop

The field station should still feel like a warm notebook base, not a management dashboard.

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not add a fourth live route yet
- do not add another bigger economy or market loop
- do not broaden the nursery into freeform base-building
- do not add a generalized cave system
- do not introduce another major parallel progression surface before this route/station chapter feels whole

## Success Signal

At the end of this phase, the game should feel less like "a lot of good systems" and more like "a few cohesive field-adventure chapters" that begin at the station, play through a clear route, and end with a satisfying sense of completion.
