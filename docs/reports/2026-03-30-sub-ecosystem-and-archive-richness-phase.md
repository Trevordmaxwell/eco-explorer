# Sub-Ecosystem And Archive Richness Phase

## Summary

After the current comparison and close-look wave, lane 2 should keep deepening the world through authored content, but the next pass should aim at the new sub-ecosystem spaces and the growing archive surfaces instead of adding broad undirected biome sprawl.

This wave should make the newer spaces feel worth revisiting:

- old-growth pocket
- deeper cave spaces
- logged route and expedition archive surfaces
- process-backed notes and close-look candidates

## Why Now

Lane 2 has already proved it can add density safely. The next best content payoff is to make the newest spatial work feel rich as soon as it lands, rather than waiting for a later cleanup wave.

This keeps lane 2 aligned with the rest of the game by:

- supporting lane 3's new traversal spaces with content, not runtime churn
- making logged routes and expeditions feel more personal through atlas and sketchbook richness
- using existing comparison, note, close-look, and archive surfaces instead of opening new UI

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare an old-growth and cave support pack.
2. Implement that content pack using note-backed, science-safe additions.
3. Review the sub-ecosystem content pack.
4. Prepare an atlas and sketchbook archive-richness pass.
5. Implement archive-richness content inside existing surfaces.
6. Review archive richness.
7. Prepare a process-backed notes and close-look pass.
8. Implement those additions using current process, note, and close-look systems.
9. Review the process-backed richness pass.
10. Run one final quiet science-ledger and payoff sweep.
11. Review the whole follow-on wave.

## Guardrails

- Stay mostly inside `src/content/**`, science docs, and content-facing tests.
- Avoid route-board shell changes, field-station structure changes, or larger progression logic.
- Use existing note, comparison, close-look, sketchbook, and atlas surfaces.
- Keep science accuracy and kid readability as hard gates.
- Do not force a content addition just to use every system.

## Intended Result

After this wave, lane 2 should leave the game with:

- richer old-growth and cave support content
- stronger logged-season and expedition archive payoff
- more note-backed close-look and process-driven discovery depth
- cleaner science-ledger and content-quality support around the new material
