# Season Capstone And Next-Season Phase

## Summary

Lane 1 needs a fresh backlog after the first expedition wave. The next high-leverage move is not another route-board compression pass or a second full expedition card. It is a compact season-capstone phase that makes the current field season feel complete, then hands the player cleanly toward the next season.

This wave should:

- turn the three-route + `ROOT HOLLOW` body of work into one satisfying chapter
- keep the field station calm and notebook-like
- use existing route, request, nursery, and station systems instead of adding a new quest-log shell
- end with one clear next-season setup card rather than a noisy dashboard

## Why Now

The game now has enough route and expedition structure that it risks feeling like a growing stack of features instead of a complete chapter. A capstone-and-handoff pass gives the player:

- a stronger sense of completion
- a better stopping point
- a clearer reason to care about the station and nursery
- a softer bridge into future content without opening a fourth route immediately

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare one compact season-capstone notebook arc.
2. Implement that capstone arc using existing route, request, journal, and station surfaces.
3. Review whether the capstone feels like a chapter ending instead of a chore list.
4. Prepare a tiny season archive and next-season handoff pattern.
5. Implement the archive and handoff.
6. Review readability and tone.
7. Prepare a small nursery-backed support pass for the capstone and expedition loop.
8. Implement that support pass without turning the nursery into a bigger economy lane.
9. Review the nursery-backed capstone support.
10. Add one calm next-season setup card.
11. Review the overall handoff pattern.

## Guardrails

- Do not add a full quest log or chapter index.
- Do not open a second fully playable expedition card in this phase.
- Keep the field station at handheld scale and notebook tone.
- Let the nursery support the season, but do not make it the center of progression.
- Preserve the cozy field-adventure tone; the capstone should feel satisfying, not urgent.

## Intended Result

After this wave, lane 1 should leave the game with:

- one season that feels authored from start to finish
- one compact archive or closure seam
- one clear sense of what the next season is about
- stronger integration between routes, expedition, station, and nursery
