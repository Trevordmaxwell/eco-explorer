# Canopy And Cavern Crossover Phase

## Summary

Lane 3's later long-push wave should make the forest's top and bottom families feel more like one authored destination rather than two separate good ideas.

The next best gains are:

1. one compact canopy-and-cavern crossover pass
2. one follow-on that strengthens recovery or memory across that family

## Why Now

The forest already has the strongest vertical language in the project. The next highest-leverage vertical move is to make that family feel more coherently outing-like rather than just taller or deeper.

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare the crossover handoff.
2. Implement the crossover pass through current forest spaces.
3. Review whether the forest vertical family now feels more like one destination.
4. Prepare one compact recovery-or-memory follow-on.
5. Implement the follow-on without broadening the traversal shell.
6. Review whether the family is richer but still readable.

## Guardrails

- Do not add a larger forest traversal rewrite.
- Do not add another cue language.
- Reuse existing canopy, cave, and recovery seams.

## Intended Result

After this phase, the forest vertical spaces should feel more like one coherent outing family and less like separate vertical showcases.
