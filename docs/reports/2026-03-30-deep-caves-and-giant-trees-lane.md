# Deep Caves And Giant Trees Lane

## Summary

Lane 3 is the vertical-exploration lane.

Its purpose is to expand the game’s cave and tree-climbing capabilities into deeper, more magical sub-ecosystems without dragging the whole project into survival, danger, or a huge engine rewrite.

This lane should focus on three outcomes:

1. make taller climbable spaces possible
2. make deeper layered cave spaces possible
3. turn both into science-forward sub-ecosystems that still feel cozy and readable

## Why This Lane

- The project already has promising proofs in `root-hollow`, climbable trunks, and sheltered vertical traversal.
- Those systems are strong enough to justify a dedicated lane instead of isolated one-off additions.
- Bigger trees and deeper caves are a natural way to make exploration feel more game-like without adding combat or heavy economy.
- A separate lane helps protect station, route, and progression work from low-level traversal churn.

## Direction

### 1. Runtime first

Do one narrow runtime pass that expands the vertical budget for:

- taller trunk climbs
- layered tree shelves
- deeper cave chambers
- clearer vertical transitions

This should stay incremental and engine-safe.

### 2. One giant-tree chapter

Use the new vertical budget to build a first old-growth pocket:

- redwood-like in feel, not geographic perfection
- huge trunk, shelf-like branches, filtered light, epiphytes, bark life
- a little magical in scale, but still grounded and readable

### 3. One deeper cave chapter

Use the same lane to build a deeper cave extension:

- layered root and stone spaces
- moisture and filtered-light gradients
- distinct sub-areas instead of one longer tunnel

### 4. One light connective bridge

If both chapters hold up, add one optional connective move or thematic bridge so the lane starts to feel like a family of vertical exploration instead of two separate novelties.

## Guardrails

- no combat
- no fail-state cave danger loop
- no full survival system
- no giant metroidvania rewrite
- no demand for geographic perfection

The target is “fun, science-forward, realistically stylized” rather than strict simulation.

## Success Signal

At the end of this lane, the game should have:

- a real giant-tree exploration chapter
- a real deeper-cave exploration chapter
- a stable traversal foundation for future vertical sub-ecosystems
