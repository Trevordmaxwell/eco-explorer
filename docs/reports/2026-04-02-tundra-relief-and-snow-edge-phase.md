# Tundra Relief And Snow Edge Phase

## Summary

Lane 3's next later wave should give the tundra branch a stronger vertical and sub-ecosystem identity without turning it into a harsh platforming space.

The next best gains are:

1. one compact tundra relief pass
2. one snow-edge or thaw-edge follow-on that makes the inland top end feel more varied

## Why Now

Treeline is gaining its own stronger vertical identity, and the tundra side now wants a small but real counterpart so the inland branch feels like more than a flat endpoint.

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare the tundra relief handoff.
2. Implement the relief pass with one compact terrain or shelter beat.
3. Review whether the tundra now feels more distinct and still calm.
4. Prepare one compact snow-edge follow-on.
5. Implement the follow-on inside the same visual and readability budget.
6. Review whether the top end now feels richer without harsher traversal.

## Guardrails

- Keep the vertical gain modest.
- Avoid making tundra harsh, maze-like, or overly sparse.
- Reuse thaw, snow-edge, and low-growth language instead of inventing another cue layer.

## Intended Result

After this phase, the inland top end should feel more varied and memorable while staying readable and gentle.
