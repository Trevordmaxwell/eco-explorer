# Season Pages And Expeditions Phase

## Summary

The current three-route station shell is now at its safe density. Even after replay and season-wrap work, the next growth step should not be “compress a fourth route onto the same board.”

The next larger phase should do two things:

1. add a new field-station pattern that can grow beyond the current three-route board
2. use that new pattern to launch one deeper expedition-style chapter built around the live forest cave and climbable exploration work

This is the cleanest way to make the game feel more game-like without drifting into a dashboard, heavy economy, or genre pivot.

## Why This Phase

- The route board is working, but it is full.
- The game now has real traversal identity in `forest`, `treeline`, `tundra`, and the front-half beach pocket.
- The newly expanded `root-hollow` cave and climbable forest spaces are strong raw material for a more exploration-forward chapter.
- The project now needs a growth pattern, not more compression.

## Direction

### 1. Season pages, not more board compression

The field station should evolve into a slightly broader but still calm structure:

- keep one current active-season page
- keep nursery secondary
- keep atlas/archive visible but not dominant
- make room for one special expedition chapter without cramming four or five equal routes into the same tiny board

This should feel like a notebook turning a page, not like a control panel gaining tabs.

### 2. One deeper expedition chapter

Use the new station pattern to launch one deeper exploration chapter built around the forest’s cave and climbable spaces.

Best fit:

- a forest-first expedition through `root-hollow`, `log-run`, and upper shelf or canopy-adjacent spaces
- route or beat structure should emphasize moisture, shelter, height, and where life clusters

The expedition should feel:

- a little longer than the current route beats
- still calm and kid-friendly
- more exploration-forward than the standard route board

### 3. Content and support around the expedition

Once the expedition shell lands, deepen it with:

- cave- and canopy-adjacent discoveries
- one or two notebook supports
- one tiny field-kit or station-side support only if needed

Do not add broad inventory, danger, combat, or a survival loop.

## What Not To Do Yet

- do not add combat or fail states
- do not turn expeditions into a survival loop
- do not add a broad inventory or loot layer
- do not turn the station into a dashboard with many equal tabs
- do not add a fifth live route before this pattern proves out

## Success Signal

At the end of this phase, the player should feel that Eco Explorer has grown from “three nice routes” into “a field season with one deeper special expedition,” and the field station should feel ready for future growth without losing its calm handheld identity.
