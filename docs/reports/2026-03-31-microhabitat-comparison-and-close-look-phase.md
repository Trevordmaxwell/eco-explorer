# Microhabitat Comparison And Close-Look Phase

## Summary

After the active microhabitat richness and archive payoff wave, lane 2 should spend one more compact content-owned pass making those new spaces feel more distinct through comparison and close-look support.

The focus should be narrow:

- one small comparison-ready pair or trio tied to old-growth or cave-adjacent spaces
- one or two visual-first close-look additions
- no new systems or shell growth

## Why Now

Lane 2's current reactivated wave will make the spaces denser and more memorable. The best follow-on is not a broader content flood. It is one compact pass that helps those discoveries teach more pattern and texture inside the existing journal and inspect seams.

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare one microhabitat comparison and close-look follow-on pack.
2. Implement that pack.
3. Review whether the new spaces now feel more distinct, teachable, and collectible without pushing lane 2 into broader engine work.

## Guardrails

- Keep the work content-owned.
- Reuse existing comparison and close-look surfaces.
- Prefer a few strong microhabitat contrasts over a broad content swell.
- Do not reopen route, traversal, or station runtime.

## Intended Result

After this phase, the newer microhabitats should not just feel full. They should feel more different from one another in ways the player can notice, collect, and remember.
