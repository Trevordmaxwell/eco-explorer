# 2026-03-30 Field-Season Routes Phase

## Recommendation

The next highest-leverage move is a calm route-progression layer.

The project now has enough working systems that the player can explore, compare, travel, survey, buy a first movement upgrade, and notice living-world change. What it still needs is a stronger medium-term reason to keep moving through the world after the first guided loop is over.

The best next phase is a field-station-centered `field season board` with one live authored route and a few clearly future routes behind it.

## Why This Is The Right Shift

The current build is already good at:

- moment-to-moment exploration
- science-safe inspection and journaling
- short authored requests
- one satisfying early loop
- gentle upgrades and continuity

It is still thinner at:

- giving the player a reason to keep going after the first loop resolves
- making the field station feel like a home base instead of only a short stop
- turning the five-biome world into a longer authored season rather than a collection of strong slices

That makes the next leverage a structure problem, not a polish problem.

## Recommended Order

1. field-season board and route progression v1
2. route-aligned content fuel pack
3. treeline traversal shelter proof
4. one route reward or utility unlock beyond the current movement pair
5. inland process moments and continuity support for revisits

## Phase 1. Field-Season Board And Route Progression V1

Build one small field-station layer that introduces the idea of a season route.

Best shape:

- the station gets one compact `Field season` board or notebook page
- only one route is live at first
- the route reuses existing notebook requests, comparison pages, corridor travel, and journal states instead of replacing them
- the route spans multiple biomes and ends with one calm acknowledgment plus a clear next direction

Strong first route:

- shelter and moisture across `beach`, `coastal-scrub`, and `forest`

Why:

- those biomes already have the richest authored support
- the current guided loop already points toward them
- the route can teach ecosystem change through adjacent habitats rather than disconnected checklists

## Phase 2. Route-Aligned Content Fuel Pack

Once the route shell exists, feed it with content that makes it feel authored rather than thin.

Best additions:

- one more strong route-supporting discovery in each touched biome
- one more shared-species or comparison anchor tied to the route theme
- one more notebook or ecosystem-note pairing that helps the route teach pattern, shelter, or moisture

This is more useful than another totally new mechanic because the route layer will immediately consume the extra content.

## Phase 3. Treeline Traversal Shelter Proof

After the coastal route holds, deepen the inland/alpine half of the world.

Best next traversal candidate:

- `treeline`

Why:

- it teaches exposure and shelter clearly
- it gives the player a new movement-feeling biome instead of repeating forest/coastal cover
- it sets up later alpine and tundra route work naturally

The proof should stay short, forgiving, and authored:

- rock step
- snow-pocket shelter
- bent-conifer or stone overhang detour

## Phase 4. One Route Reward Or Utility Unlock

Only after the route and second traversal proof are working should the station gain one more reward.

Preferred direction:

- a fieldwork utility
- a comfort or convenience unlock
- a notebook or travel support improvement

Avoid:

- large economy escalation
- collectible selling
- reward structures that feel extractive or grindy

## Phase 5. Inland Process Moments

The next living-world lift should happen where route revisits will actually feel different.

Best first targets:

- forest moisture shift
- treeline frost or exposure shift
- tundra thaw or bloom emphasis

Those will land better after the route layer gives the player a reason to come back.

## Scout Work That Is Worth Running In Parallel

The scout can help a lot here without blocking the main lane:

1. act-two beat sheet for the first field-season board
2. treeline traversal shelter concepts and content matrix
3. tier-three route reward ethics and utility framing

That keeps planning ahead while the main agent implements the current phase cleanly.

## Durable Rules

- The next phase should make the game feel longer and more intentional, not just busier.
- Route progression should stay notebook-first and field-station-centered, not a giant quest log.
- The field station should remain a science-safe base, not a sell-market.
- New rewards should help observation and traversal, not turn the game into a grind loop.
- The first route should stay tightly authored and biome-specific before the game grows into broader seasonal structures.

## Queue Outcome

- promote one main implementation lane for the field-season board and route progression v1
- promote one scout lane for the act-two beat sheet
- add two more scout downtime lanes so the next traversal and reward phase are already grounded
- keep later content, traversal, reward, and inland-process expansion parked behind critique gates
