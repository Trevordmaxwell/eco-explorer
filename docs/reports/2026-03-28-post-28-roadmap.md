# Post-28 Roadmap

Date: 2026-03-28
Status: Recommended next-phase plan after the coastal science cleanup

## Why This Is The Right Pause Point

`main-28` is a good natural boundary.

The project now has:

- a stable five-biome ecosystem chain
- world-map travel and doorway transitions
- a readable multi-biome journal
- an ecosystem-note teaching layer
- a clipboard-first field guide
- durable tests, docs, queueing, and source-ledger habits

That means the next best improvements are no longer mostly bug fixes. They are product-shaping choices.

## Recommendation

Do not jump straight into the broad parked living-world ideas yet.

The highest-value order from here is:

1. Make the world feel fuller with a measured content-density pass.
2. Add a very light progression layer so discovery has more shape.
3. Only then choose one living-world slice worth making real.

## Phase 2A: Content Density

This should be the next implementation phase.

Why:

- it makes the game feel more complete immediately
- it strengthens the educational value without changing the core interaction model
- it is low-risk compared with larger system work

Target outcome:

- each live biome gains a modest but real second layer of authored discoveries
- the five-biome route feels worth revisiting instead of like one pass through sparse slices
- the journal totals become more satisfying without turning into a checklist wall

Good scope:

- add at least two new discoverable entries per live biome
- bias toward one habitat-defining plant/landmark and one animal or collectible per biome
- keep all facts science-safe and age-readable
- update spawn tables, tests, and source notes as part of the same pass

## Phase 2B: Lightweight Progression

This should follow the content pass, not precede it.

Why:

- progression works better when there is enough content to support it
- the current game is cozy and open-ended; the next layer should add shape without turning into quests

Recommended direction:

- journal-first milestones
- tiny unlock moments
- map or journal feedback that recognizes observation progress

Avoid:

- quest logs
- explicit chores
- score systems
- anything that punishes free exploration

## Phase 2C: Living-World Slice

Only do this after content density and light progression are both stable.

Why:

- this is the first point where the project starts to become a bigger expansion instead of a refinement pass
- the parked living-world ideas are promising, but too broad to activate as one blob

Approved later features:

- one simple day-part palette and ambient-state system
- biome-specific weather ambience
- naturalist-style observational prompts
- a lightweight field partner / companion layer
- related ambient ecosystem indicators that support those systems

Bad candidate:

- turning on day/night, weather, ecosystem pulse, field partner, and direct AI mode all at once

## Phase 3: Depth And Authorship

After the current phase-two fixes and the first living-world slices, the best long-term expansions are the ones that deepen pattern, change, comparison, and player authorship.

Approved later directions:

- gentle seasons and phenology variation
- notebook-style relationship prompts instead of achievement quests
- shared-species comparison pages
- an art-forward sketchbook mode
- sparse biome soundscapes
- occasional close-look inspect vignettes

Best guiding question:

- does this feature help the player notice difference, repetition, shelter, exposure, or change over time?

Bad candidate:

- adding systems just because they sound game-like
- generic achievement ladders
- crafting loops that pull focus away from observation
- loud always-on UI layers

## What To Keep Saying No To

- combat
- fail states
- backend accounts
- heavy HUD growth
- giant open-world rewrites
- direct AI mode before the clipboard-first field-guide path clearly earns it

## Queue Outcome

- queue a content-density implementation pass first
- queue a scout handoff for lightweight progression in parallel
- keep the approved living-world slice work parked until those two are done
- let the scout prepare later depth systems as planning-only sidecar work without activating implementation early
