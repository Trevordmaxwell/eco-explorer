# Giant-Tree And Deep-Pocket Phase

## Summary

Lane 3's next vertical wave should turn the strongest forest vertical spaces into more destination-like exploration.

The best next shape is:

1. one giant-tree continuation that feels like a true climb destination
2. one deeper cave pocket that feels secret and recoverable rather than harsh

This should keep lane 3 moving toward expedition-grade spaces without opening combat, survival, or a denser traversal HUD.

## Why Now

The current canopy and cavern family is coherent and readable. That means the next gain should come from place quality, not more proof-of-concept runtime.

One stronger giant-tree branch and one deeper cave pocket will do more for wonder and place memory than another generic height increase or tunnel extension.

## Recommended Order

1. Prepare the giant-tree destination continuation.
2. Implement the giant-tree continuation.
3. Review it for readability, wonder, and recovery.
4. Prepare the deeper cave pocket follow-on.
5. Implement the deeper cave pocket.
6. Review whether the deeper cave now feels magical and fair instead of merely lower.

## Guardrails

- Keep the spaces cozy and recoverable.
- Reuse the current tiny cue language instead of inventing a new one.
- Avoid widening into a large cave network or traversal shell.
- Redwood-like scale cheats are acceptable when they improve play and atmosphere.

## Intended Result

After this phase, the forest vertical family should contain one climb that feels special and one cave pocket that feels like a real hidden place, both still readable on the current handheld screen.
