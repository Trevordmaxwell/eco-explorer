# Playtest Comprehension Rubric

Use this rubric when an adult watches a kid play the current Eco Explorer alpha arc. The goal is to learn what the player understands, remembers, feels, and struggles with, not to collect feature requests or score the child.

## Privacy And Tone

- Do not record a child's full name, school, address, contact information, voice, face, or other identifying details in this repo.
- Do not ask the child to rate themselves, rank their skill, or defend why they got stuck.
- Keep questions warm and open. The best notes capture what the player noticed in their own words.
- Park "cool idea" feature requests separately from comprehension blockers.

## Adult Setup

Before the session:

- Use a fresh save or a named packet `131` save snapshot when one is available.
- Tell the player: "Please explore, notice things, and say what you are wondering about. There are no wrong answers."
- Let the player ask for help, but mark where help was needed.
- Keep the session focused on one arc slice or one full completed-arc run, not an endless free-play survey.

Suggested observation anchors:

- first beach outing and `Shore Shelter`
- station return and support choice
- Root Hollow / forest expedition memory
- transition from filed season into `High Pass`
- filed `High Pass` and post-filed settling

## Quick Triage

Use these labels after the session:

- `Green`: the player understood the moment or recovered without adult explanation.
- `Yellow`: the player eventually understood, but needed rereading, wandering, or one adult nudge.
- `Red`: the player stayed confused, mislearned the science, or could not continue without repeated help.

Treat repeated `Red` or repeated `Yellow` notes as stronger evidence than one-off preferences.

## Child Prompts

Ask only a few. Let the player answer casually.

- What place do you remember most?
- What helped something survive there?
- What did you learn about how living things use shelter, water, weather, or seasons?
- Where did you know what to do next?
- Where did you need help?
- What did you want to show someone else?
- What did the game make you curious about in real nature?

## Science Takeaways

Look for relationship language. Good evidence can be simple:

- "The grass helped hold sand."
- "The forest was wetter and cooler."
- "Short summers make tundra plants hurry."
- "The rocks made little sheltered spots."
- "Some things showed up in more than one place but had different jobs."

Watch for science misses:

- living things treated as decorations only
- lichens, plants, animals, and landmarks getting mixed together in a misleading way
- survival facts remembered as magic powers
- routes remembered as chores without an ecosystem reason
