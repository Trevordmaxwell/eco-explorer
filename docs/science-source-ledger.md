# Science Source Ledger

Date: 2026-03-30
Status: Updated after `ECO-20260330-main-102`

## Purpose

This ledger gives future content passes one compact place to check:

- whether a live or planned entry already has a credible source trail
- whether its current fact wording is safe to keep
- whether the larger biome chain still makes geographic sense

It is intentionally compact. It is not a full classroom bibliography.

## Source Rules

- Prefer primary or official references such as USDA, USFS, NPS, USFWS, Cornell Lab, or Animal Diversity Web.
- Treat short kid-facing fact text as an interpretation of the sources, not a direct quote.
- Mark entries `Watch` when the science is usable but wording, taxonomy, or region fit still needs care.
- Mark entries `Risk` when the current ecosystem chain would become misleading without a design decision.

## Status Key

- `Verified`: good enough to scaffold or keep with current wording
- `Watch`: usable, but taxonomy, wording, or habitat framing should stay conservative
- `Risk`: real accuracy or geography issue that should be resolved before the five-biome chain is treated as one continuous branch

## Cross-Biome Risk Summary

The current approved gradient is:

`beach -> coastal-scrub -> forest -> treeline -> tundra`

The coastal side now follows a Pacific branch.

- The live beach and live `Coastal Scrub` biome now center on Pacific-coherent species such as American dunegrass, Pacific littleneck clam, Pacific sand crab, Pacific wax myrtle, shore pine, Nootka rose, and salmonberry.
- That keeps the left side aligned with the already-Pacific forest and live treeline branch.
- The remaining guardrails are to avoid reintroducing Atlantic coastal anchors and to keep any introduced Pacific-coast species framed clearly instead of quietly teaching them as native dune defaults.
